Core philosophy

"The Profit of Progress"

Profitability is the engine for sustainable social impact. Operational efficiency, diversity, and optimal human-system design directly yield superior unit economics.

Design judgment

The hardest part of my job has never been designing the system. It's been stopping other people from overdesigning it.

In 20 years, the pattern is always the same: teams hardcode business rules that should be configurable. They build features nobody asked for because the architecture allows it. They optimize for technical elegance instead of the person who has to use the thing at 4pm on a Friday.

My value isn't building the most sophisticated system. It's building the right one — maintainable, human-centered, and designed to survive the first time someone who wasn't in the room makes a change.

Systems thinking

I don't just solve for the immediate problem. I identify patterns and design systems that can be reused, modularized, and productized.

Every engagement becomes input into a broader system. I consistently move from bespoke consulting to platform thinking — turning one-off solutions into configurable architectures and scalable products. This is the bridge between consulting and venture creation.

Decision system design

I design decision systems, not just solutions.

Most organizations fail because decision-making is inconsistent, unclear, or undocumented. I make decision logic explicit — defining what decisions exist, who owns them, what inputs are required, and how outcomes are measured. This ensures organizations can operate effectively long after I leave.

How I sell

I tell the story wearing the user's hat.

I drive conversion by diagnosing what customers actually need — often before they can articulate it — and aligning solutions directly to their operational pain, decision triggers, and desired outcomes. I translate complex technical architecture into language that lands with executives, evaluators, and end users.

Then I align the entire team on why the solution is excellent, how to communicate that to the customer, and how to integrate it into existing operations with the least friction. That's why every competitive proposal I've led has made the shortlist.

How I operate

Diagnosis first. I do not start with solutions. I start by identifying the root problem.

Structure before action. I convert ambiguity into frameworks, definitions, and scoped boundaries.

Explicit over implicit. I make assumptions and decision logic visible before execution begins.

Sequence matters. The right work done in the wrong order fails.

Reusability as a standard. Every solution is evaluated for its ability to scale or productize.

Clarity as leverage. Clear thinking, clear language, and clear scope reduce cost, risk, and time.

About

Operator. Builder. Founder.

First-generation American. Parents from Sierra Leone. I started my career managing street canvassing teams across Manhattan and Brooklyn — recruiting, training, and motivating distributed teams in high-turnover political campaigns. That's where I learned to read people, build teams under pressure, and understand that every operational problem is a human problem first.

From there I moved into accounting and financial reporting, then into the Salesforce ecosystem, where I spent over a decade not as a coder but as the person who understood the business, the users, and the politics — and translated that into systems designed for the comfort of the people using them.

I've worked across 15+ industries, in the US, UK, Germany, Australia, and West Africa. I've led pre-sales and competitive bidding that achieved a 100% shortlist rate across two firms. I've been the person deployed into failing programs to diagnose what's broken — and the diagnosis was rarely just the technology.

I ask the right question, in the right room, at the right time, and the whole system reorganizes around the answer.

Now I operate on three tracks simultaneously: I operate — walking into organizations to fix the human systems that make everything else work. I build — designing, architecting, and launching platforms, products, and technical solutions from zero. What sets me apart is the combination of design judgment (stopping teams from overdesigning), systems thinking (turning one-off consulting into reusable, scalable platforms), decision system design (making organizational logic explicit so it survives my departure), and narrative ability (telling the story from the user's perspective so the right people say yes).

I'm AI-native — not experimenting, operating. I use Claude, Gemini, and local models daily for operational design, compliance analysis, and process automation. I design AI governance frameworks for regulated industries where getting it wrong has real consequences for real people.
