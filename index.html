Design judgment

The hardest part of my job has never been designing the system. It's been stopping other people from overdesigning it.

In 20 years, the pattern is always the same: teams hardcode business rules that should be configurable. They build features nobody asked for because the architecture allows it. They optimize for technical elegance instead of the person who has to use the thing at 4pm on a Friday.

My value isn't building the most sophisticated system. It's building the right one — maintainable, human-centered, and designed to survive the first time someone who wasn't in the room makes a change.

About

Operator. Builder. Founder.

I started my career managing street canvassing teams across Manhattan and Brooklyn — recruiting, training, and motivating distributed teams in high-turnover political campaigns. That's where I learned to read people, build teams under pressure, and understand that every operational problem is a human problem first.

From there I moved into accounting and financial reporting, then into the Salesforce ecosystem, where I spent over a decade not as a coder but as the person who understood the business, the users, and the politics — and translated that into systems designed for the comfort of the people using them.

I've worked across 15+ industries, in the US, UK, Germany, Australia, and West Africa. I've led pre-sales and competitive bidding that achieved a 100% shortlist rate across two firms. I've been the person deployed into failing programs to diagnose what's broken — and the diagnosis was rarely just the technology.

Now I operate on three tracks simultaneously: I operate — walking into organizations to fix the human systems that make everything else work. I build — designing and shipping platforms, products, and technical solutions. And I found — creating Kombra Group as a venture studio and Africa Tomorrow as a sustainable development initiative in Sierra Leone.

Most people are one of these. The combination is what makes the work I do — and the ventures I build — different.

What sets my technical work apart is design judgment. I've spent 20 years watching teams hardcode business rules that should be configurable, build features nobody asked for, and optimize for elegance instead of the person using the system at 4pm on a Friday. I mentor the teams I lead to make better choices: surface configuration panels, consider long-term maintainability, right-size every solution, and never over-engineer something that creates more debt than it solves.

And I know how to sell what I build. I translate complex technical architecture into the user's story — explaining how a caseworker's workflow improves, how a citizen's experience is transformed, how constituents are better served for less cost, and how faith in the system is restored. I align entire teams on why the solution is excellent, how to communicate that to the customer, and how to integrate it with the least friction. That narrative ability is why every competitive proposal I've led has made the shortlist.

Languages: English natively. Conversational German and Spanish — enough to support bilingual teams and operate across cultures.
