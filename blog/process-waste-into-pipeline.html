Turning Process Waste into Pipeline

Michelle Bangura · April 2026 · 3 min read

Every enterprise has operational waste hiding in plain sight. Tickets that get routed three times before someone resolves them. Reports that take 4 hours to compile from data that already exists in a system nobody checks. Approvals that sit in someone's inbox for 72 hours because the escalation path isn't defined.

Most consultants see this waste and sell a "process optimization" engagement. That's fine. But it misses the bigger opportunity.

Process waste is a sales narrative.

If you're selling AI-powered operations tools, every inefficiency in your prospect's current system is a buying trigger. The trick is making the prospect SEE it — quantifying the cost of what they've been tolerating because "that's just how it works here."

Here's the methodology:

First, identify the waste. Not from a boardroom. From the floor. Shadow the IT team for a day. Watch the ticket queue. Count the touches. Time the resolution. Ask the person doing the work: "What takes the longest? What's the dumbest part of your day?"

Second, quantify it. That 4-hour report costs the organization $200 every time it runs. That ticket that gets routed three times before resolution adds 45 minutes of labor per incident. At 500 incidents per month, that's 375 hours — 2.3 full-time employees doing nothing but routing. Put that number in front of a CFO.

Third, map the waste to your solution. "Our platform eliminates the routing step entirely. AI classifies the ticket at intake. That's 2.3 FTEs worth of productivity returned to your team. At an average fully-loaded cost of $85K per FTE, that's $195K in annual savings from one feature."

That's not a feature pitch. That's an ROI conversation. And ROI conversations close deals.

The companies winning in enterprise AI sales right now are the ones that diagnose operational waste and translate it into a buying decision. The diagnosis IS the pipeline. The waste IS the wedge.

Stop selling features. Start quantifying the cost of doing nothing. The math does the closing for you.
